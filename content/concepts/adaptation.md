# Adaptation

> Adaptation is where the foundational chain closes: an organization changes its structure, connections, and flow while the persistent organizational continuity that its identity is tracked against remains intact.

## Definition

Adaptation is the continuous process through which an organization modifies its structure, connections, or flow in response to feedback while maintaining persistent existence.

Within Connection Theory, adaptation enables organizations to remain compatible with continuously changing reality.

---

## Intuition

Persistent organizations cannot survive by remaining unchanged.

Living organisms adapt to environmental changes.

Human societies adjust their institutions.

Artificial intelligence systems improve through learning.

Although the mechanisms differ, each organization continuously modifies itself in order to maintain long-term persistence.

---

## Why It Matters

Reality continuously changes.

Without adaptation, organizations gradually become incompatible with their environment and eventually lose their ability to persist.

Adaptation therefore provides the mechanism through which organizations maintain long-term compatibility with reality.

---

## Role in Connection Theory

Adaptation represents the outcome of continuous interaction among organization, connection, flow, constraint, and feedback.

It completes the foundational conceptual framework and provides the bridge toward higher-level theories, including learning, intelligence, and long-term evolution.

---

## Examples

Examples of adaptation include:

- Biological adaptation to changing environments
- Learning in artificial intelligence systems
- Organizational restructuring within companies
- Behavioral adjustment in human societies
- Ecological adaptation within ecosystems

Although these adaptations occur at different scales, each enables organizations to maintain persistence under changing conditions.
