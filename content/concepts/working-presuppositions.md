# Working Presuppositions

> These are methodological commitments adopted for the purpose of doing research. They are not claims about what reality ultimately is.

## Definition

Working Presuppositions are the minimal set of methodological commitments Connection Theory adopts in order to move from "objective reality exists" (Layer 0) to a workable research stance.

They are called *working* presuppositions deliberately: they are held because research cannot proceed without them, not because they have been independently proven. They are open to revision if a better working stance is found, but they are not treated as live questions to be re-litigated in every study - they are the floor a study stands on.

---

## Intuition

Layer 0 establishes only that something is real, independent of any observer. That premise alone gives a researcher nothing to do. It says nothing about how one might come to know anything about that reality, or how confident one should be in what one comes to know.

Working Presuppositions fill exactly that gap, and nothing more. They say: knowledge of reality is obtained by observing it, and whatever models are built from those observations should be treated as revisable, not final. Both of these are commitments about *how to do research*, not new claims about *what reality is made of*. That second kind of claim belongs to later layers.

---

## Why It Matters

Every research program presupposes some method for connecting claims to evidence, whether or not it says so explicitly.

Leaving these presuppositions unstated invites two problems. First, it becomes unclear which parts of a theory are meant to be tested against observation and which parts are simply assumed. Second, it becomes easy to mistake a working method for a metaphysical conclusion - to start believing that because a model has worked well so far, it must be describing reality exactly as it is, rather than describing reality as well as current observation and encoding allow.

Stating the presuppositions explicitly keeps both of these separate: what is assumed in order to do the work, and what is concluded as a result of doing it.

---

## Role in Connection Theory

Working Presuppositions occupy Layer 1, directly above Reality (Layer 0) and directly below Observation (Layer 2).

They convert the bare premise of Layer 0 into a usable starting point for everything that follows. Without them, Layer 2 through Layer 7 would have no justification for treating observation as informative or models as improvable.

Three presuppositions are stated here as the general methodological minimum, applicable regardless of what is being studied. A fourth, more specific presupposition - concerning the continuity of reality within the observational scope Connection Theory adopts - is documented separately, since it makes a more substantive commitment than the three below and deserves its own explanation. See Continuous Reality.

---

## The Presuppositions

### WP1 - Research Proceeds Under the Assumption of Objective Reality

This restates the Layer 0 premise at the point where it becomes operative: research proceeds as though there is something real for its claims to be about, independent of the researcher's beliefs or descriptions.

Layer 0 states that objective reality exists. WP1 is not a repetition of that claim - it is the methodological act of adopting it as the ground on which research is conducted. The two are related but distinct: one names a premise, the other names research's commitment to proceed on that premise.

### WP2 - Scientific Knowledge Is Obtained Through Observation

Whatever can be known about reality within this framework is known by observing it, directly or instrumentally, rather than by pure reasoning detached from observation, or by appeal to authority.

This presupposition does not claim that observation delivers reality unfiltered - Layer 2 and Layer 3 (Observation and Encoding) address exactly why it does not. It claims only that observation, however imperfect, is the route by which knowledge is obtained, and that no other route is treated as a substitute for it within this framework.

### WP3 - Scientific Models Are Continuously Revised Through Observation and Validation

No model produced within this framework is treated as final. Every model remains open to revision as new observations are made and validated against it.

This presupposition is what keeps Working Presuppositions distinct from dogma: a working presupposition can itself be reconsidered if it stops producing a coherent, workable account of what is observed - though, as noted above, this happens rarely and deliberately, not on a case-by-case basis within ordinary research.

---

## Examples

- A biologist who has never articulated WP2 still designs experiments, because the presupposition is already operating beneath the practice, whether or not it is named.
- A theory that refuses to revise itself in light of new, validated observations has implicitly abandoned WP3, regardless of what else it claims to be doing scientifically.
- Two researchers can disagree sharply about what a particular observation means while still sharing WP1-WP3 completely - the disagreement lives at a higher layer, not at this one.
