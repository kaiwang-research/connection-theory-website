# Reality

> This layer names the premise that objective reality exists. It is not a description of what reality is.

## Definition

Reality, at the foundation of Connection Theory, refers to the single premise that objective reality exists. No further ontological commitments are made at this layer.

This is the only claim made at this layer. Connection Theory does not attempt to define what reality ultimately consists of, whether it is continuous or discrete, deterministic or probabilistic, physical or otherwise. Those are separate questions, addressed - and only ever partially, provisionally - at later layers of the framework.

---

## Intuition

Every theory has to start somewhere. Most theories start much further along than this: they start by already assuming what kind of things exist, how they behave, and what counts as evidence.

Connection Theory tries to start from the most minimal point possible: before assuming continuity, before assuming structure, before assuming dynamism, before assuming that objects or organizations exist at all - there is simply the premise that something is there, independent of any observer's description of it.

Everything else the framework says - that reality is continuous within a given observational scope, that it is best understood as dynamic organization, that persistent systems can be studied within it - is built on top of this premise. None of it is contained in the premise itself.

---

## Why It Matters

If a theory does not state its starting premise explicitly, that premise tends to get smuggled in through later definitions, where it becomes much harder to inspect or question.

By isolating "objective reality exists" as a separate, minimal layer, Connection Theory keeps this premise visible and keeps it separate from the much more specific claims made later - claims about continuity, organization, and dynamism, which are working presuppositions and framework principles, not restatements of this layer.

This separation also clarifies what kind of project Connection Theory is. It is not a project that argues reality exists - that is simply adopted as the starting point. It is a project about how organized, persistent systems can be studied and described within that reality, given that any description of it is necessarily obtained through observation and encoding, and is therefore always partial.

---

## Role in Connection Theory

Reality occupies Layer 0 - the base of Connection Theory's foundation, beneath Working Presuppositions, Observation, Encoding, Abstraction, and Framework.

Nothing about how reality is structured is claimed here. Structure enters only at Layer 1, where Working Presuppositions state the minimal methodological commitments needed to do research at all (that scientific knowledge comes from observation, and that models are revised through it), and further at Layer 5, where Framework states a specific, richer orientation toward reality - that it is best understood as continuous dynamic organization.

Layer 0 supports all of this without asserting any of it. Its only job is to mark the point beyond which Connection Theory does not go: the theory does not attempt to establish why reality exists, or what it is composed of at its most fundamental level. It takes reality's existence as given, and turns its attention entirely to the question of how organized, persistent systems can be studied within it.

---

## Examples

- A physicist, a biologist, and a sociologist disagree constantly about what their respective subject matters are made of, but all three presuppose that there is something out there for their observations to be about. That shared presupposition is Layer 0.
- A skeptical position such as solipsism, which doubts whether anything exists independently of one's own mind, would deny Layer 0 outright. Connection Theory does not attempt to refute this position; it simply does not build on it. Layer 0 is the point at which Connection Theory and radical skepticism part ways.
- Discovering that a phenomenon behaves differently than expected does not threaten Layer 0. It only ever revises claims made at higher layers - the encoding used to observe it, or the model built from that encoding - never the premise that there was something real to observe in the first place.
