# Feedback

> Feedback is how an organization keeps its persistent organizational continuity (Framework Principle 2) intact while the constraints and flows around it keep changing.

## Definition

Feedback is the process through which the outcomes of an organization's actions influence its future organization, behavior, or flow.

Within Connection Theory, feedback enables organizations to continuously adjust themselves in response to internal states and external reality.

---

## Intuition

Persistent organizations do not simply act; they continuously respond to the consequences of their actions.

Living organisms regulate internal processes through biological feedback.

Neural systems continuously adjust their activity based on sensory input.

Human organizations evaluate outcomes and modify future decisions.

Artificial intelligence systems update their parameters according to observed performance.

Feedback allows organizations to learn from experience rather than repeatedly producing the same behavior.

---

## Why It Matters

Without feedback, organizations cannot reliably improve or maintain themselves over time.

Feedback provides the mechanism through which organizations detect differences between expected and actual outcomes and adjust accordingly.

It therefore enables learning, self-regulation, and long-term persistence.

---

## Role in Connection Theory

Feedback connects organizational behavior with future organizational change.

It transforms static organizational processes into adaptive ones and provides the foundation for:

- Adaptation
- Learning
- Continuous organizational refinement

Within Connection Theory, persistent adaptation depends on continuous feedback between organizations and reality.

---

## Examples

Examples of feedback include:

- Homeostatic regulation in living organisms
- Learning in neural networks
- Performance evaluation within organizations
- Error correction in engineering systems
- Reinforcement learning in artificial intelligence

Although implemented differently, each allows organizations to modify future behavior based on previous outcomes.
